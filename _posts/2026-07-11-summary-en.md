QuadRF, an open-source 4x4 MIMO software-defined radio platform powered by a Raspberry Pi 5, has been demonstrated to detect drones in flight and visualize WiFi signals through walls using beamforming and phased-array technology. This democratizes advanced RF sensing capabilities previously limited to government agencies, enabling hobbyists and researchers to perform drone detection, through-wall WiFi mapping, and spectrum analysis with an affordable, open-source tool. The QuadRF uses four coherent transmit/receive channels, swappable dual-polarization antennas, and an FPGA with picosecond-level timing for precise beamforming. It operates as a real-time RF camera with a browser-based interface.

hackernews · speckx · Jul 10, 15:59 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48861717)

**Background**: Software-defined radio (SDR) allows radio communication to be implemented in software rather than hardware. Phased-array antennas use multiple elements to steer beams electronically, enabling direction finding and imaging. QuadRF combines these technologies with a Raspberry Pi 5 to create an accessible RF imaging platform.

## [EU threatens Meta with fines over addictive features](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/eu-threatens-meta-with-fines-over-addictive-features-on-facebook-and-instagram/) ⭐️ 7.0/10

The European Commission has formally warned Meta that its use of infinite scroll, autoplay, push notifications, and personalized recommendation algorithms on Facebook and Instagram may violate the Digital Services Act (DSA), potentially leading to significant fines. This action could set a precedent for regulating addictive design patterns across the tech industry, forcing platforms to redesign core user experiences to comply with EU law and protect users from compulsive usage. The DSA imposes strict obligations on Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) like Meta, which must conduct risk assessments and mitigate systemic risks such as addictive behaviors. Non-compliance can result in fines of up to 6% of global annual turnover.

rss · TechCrunch · Jul 10, 14:19

**Background**: The Digital Services Act (DSA) is an EU regulation that came into force in 2022, establishing comprehensive rules for digital services accountability, content moderation, and platform transparency. It applies to all online intermediaries, with stricter requirements for platforms with over 45 million monthly active users in the EU. Infinite scrolling and personalized recommendation algorithms are common design patterns that can encourage prolonged engagement, potentially leading to addictive usage.
