## [HDD-RoPE: A New Positional Encoding Outperforms xPos](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1uelcm9/high_dimensional_dynamic_rotary_positional/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The author introduces High Dimensional Dynamic Rotary Positional Embedding (HDD-RoPE), a novel positional encoding method that achieves faster convergence than xPos on the TinyStories dataset. This work challenges the standard RoPE assumption that position is one-dimensional, potentially enabling transformers to learn more complex positional relationships and improving efficiency in language modeling. HDD-RoPE uses data-dependent rotation rates and can break query/key chunks into arbitrary sizes (e.g., 4), corresponding to multiple rotation axes. The open-source code and detailed math are available on GitHub.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/mikayahlevi · Jun 24, 18:16

**Background**: Rotary Positional Embedding (RoPE) encodes position by rotating pairs of query and key elements at fixed rates. xPos is an improved variant that extends RoPE with exponential decay. TinyStories is a synthetic dataset of short stories with limited vocabulary, often used for testing small language models.
